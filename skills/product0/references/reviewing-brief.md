# Reviewing the Product0 brief

## Preconditions and verdicts

Read the complete canonical brief and its current revision. Its status must be
`handoff-draft`; read referenced evidence when available.

Use only:

```text
HANDOFF_READY
NOT_READY
```

Open technical questions are acceptable. Open blocking product decisions are
not.

## Review priorities

Check strategic usefulness, evidence, provenance, type-specific depth,
proportionality, product/technical boundary, and developer usability. Reject a
brief that has no meaningful repository evidence despite available access,
reformats the request without a recommendation, presents a provisional claim as
fixed, lets routine mechanics dominate strategy, repeats itself, adds ceremonial
IDs or slices, omits required lens depth, leaves a material product decision to
the developer, or is disproportionate to its useful decisions.

Marketing briefs must cover audience, page job, positioning, hierarchy,
differentiation, proof, objections, visual direction, and conversion. Workflow
briefs must cover actors, lifecycle, authority, rules, and consequential
exceptions. The selected lens must provide equivalent depth for other types.

## Review behavior and handoff

Fix obvious editorial defects and repetition inline; do not silently repair
substantive direction. For each blocker, report the exact gap, why it matters,
whether Product0 can resolve it from existing authority, and the smallest next
action. When it can, revise the brief, increment its revision, and review again.

When it passes, summarize the executive direction, strongest evidence, fixed
decisions, recommendations, assumptions and open risks, and questions left for
technical brainstorming. Ask: `Approve this written revision for developer
handoff?` On explicit approval, set `status: handoff-ready`, record the
revision and approval date concisely, report the exact path, and stop Product0.
