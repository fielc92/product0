# Internal-operation lens

LENS_ID_INTERNAL_OPERATION

Use this lens for work that improves how employees, support teams, operations, or administrators deliver the product. The product decision is the operational outcome and its guardrails, not a screen-by-screen replacement for a current tool. Inspect the real operating workflow, handoffs, queue health, error and rework patterns, permissions, service expectations, and affected customer experience. Internal users are product users when their work determines quality, speed, safety, or consistency for customers.

## LENS_EVIDENCE_TO_INSPECT

Read runbooks, support tickets, incident reviews, current tools, process maps, role descriptions, volumes, and timing data. Separate a local workaround from a recurring system problem. Identify which customer outcome the operation protects and which parties are affected by slow, inconsistent, or unsafe work.

## LENS_RECOMMENDATIONS_TO_MAKE

Recommend the smallest operational outcome, the actor with authority, expected handoffs, meaningful exceptions, and success measures. Preserve necessary controls while removing avoidable effort. Make visible the quality or customer guardrail that prevents efficiency work from merely transferring burden to a different team or customer.

## LENS_BLOCKING_PRODUCT_DECISIONS

Escalate choices about authority, acceptable risk, customer treatment during exceptions, staffing commitments, or an enduring policy change. Package linked decisions with a recommendation, evidence or stated assumption, why the choice matters, and a minimum response. Decide delegated low-risk defaults and explain the rationale without seeking ceremony.

## LENS_IMPLEMENTATION_DETAILS_NOT_TO_ASK

Do not ask about admin table columns, queue technology, bulk-action mechanics, audit-log storage, keyboard shortcuts, validation implementation, or generic responsive behavior unless these alter authority, safety, or the customer promise. Later technical brainstorming selects those mechanisms.
