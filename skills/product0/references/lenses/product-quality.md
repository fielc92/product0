# Product-quality lens

LENS_ID_PRODUCT_QUALITY

Use this lens for reliability, usability, accessibility, performance, trust, and quality improvements that change the lived product experience. Start with the affected user outcome, rather than accepting a vague request to make the product better. Inspect customer evidence, support contacts, telemetry, defects, research, accessibility findings, and the relevant journey. Quality work should make a specific promise more dependable or easier to achieve, with guardrails against improvement for one group causing harm for another.

## LENS_EVIDENCE_TO_INSPECT

Examine where users fail, wait, abandon, make mistakes, lose confidence, or need help. Compare intended outcomes with observed behavior, and use severity, frequency, affected population, and business consequence to prioritize. Include known constraints such as assistive technology needs, low-bandwidth contexts, or critical recovery paths when the repository evidence establishes them.

## LENS_RECOMMENDATIONS_TO_MAKE

Recommend a measurable quality outcome, the journey or population in scope, the user-visible improvement, and guardrails that preserve safety and trust. Define what will remain unchanged so the initiative stays coherent. Prefer an outcome such as successful completion or understandable recovery over a purely technical target detached from user value.

## LENS_BLOCKING_PRODUCT_DECISIONS

Escalate only priority tradeoffs, acceptable degradation, a change to a core promise, or an accessibility and safety decision with material customer impact. State a recommendation, evidence or explicit assumption, why it matters, and the minimum user response. When low-risk judgment is delegated, decide and record the rationale instead of requiring needless approval.

## LENS_IMPLEMENTATION_DETAILS_NOT_TO_ASK

Do not ask about caching, rendering strategy, component primitives, test tools, browser support mechanics, loading indicators, keyboard bindings, or generic responsive behavior unless a choice materially changes the product promise. Technical brainstorming owns the design and verification mechanisms.
