# Pricing and packaging lens

LENS_ID_PRICING_PACKAGING

Use this lens for plans, entitlements, trials, add-ons, upgrades, and changes to how value is offered or charged. It frames a coherent customer value exchange instead of turning billing configuration into a product interview. Inspect the current offer, usage patterns, sales motion, support friction, competitive context, and product costs. Treat published pricing and contractual commitments as strong evidence, while recognizing that an internal SKU name may not reflect the customer mental model.

## LENS_EVIDENCE_TO_INSPECT

Review who buys, who uses, willingness-to-pay evidence, activation and renewal signals, plan adoption, entitlement boundaries, discount patterns, and the support burden caused by the current offer. Identify the customer job each tier or add-on is meant to serve and where the present packaging obscures value.

## LENS_RECOMMENDATIONS_TO_MAKE

Recommend a simple value metric, plan narrative, eligibility boundary, and upgrade path that make the offer understandable. Explain the intended segment, what each offer enables, and the non-goals that keep packaging from becoming a feature backlog. Use explicit assumptions when evidence is incomplete.

## LENS_BLOCKING_PRODUCT_DECISIONS

Ask for authority when a price, included value, grandfathering policy, trial promise, regional treatment, or customer commitment materially changes. Present at most two compact packets for a small initiative, each with Product0's recommendation, supporting evidence, significance, and minimum response.

## LENS_IMPLEMENTATION_DETAILS_NOT_TO_ASK

Do not ask about billing-provider objects, tax calculation, proration code, checkout layouts, metering schema, feature-flag implementation, or invoice format unless the choice changes a customer-facing promise. Technical brainstorming owns those details after the product direction is approved.
