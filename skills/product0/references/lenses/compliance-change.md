# Compliance-change lens

LENS_ID_COMPLIANCE_CHANGE

Use this lens when a law, regulation, contractual rule, security obligation, or policy change constrains product behavior. Product0 should translate the source requirement into a defensible product outcome without giving legal advice or inventing certainty. Inspect the authoritative obligation, its applicability, current behavior, affected users, deadlines, audit evidence, and the practical consequences of non-compliance. Keep the work proportionate: a compliance need does not automatically justify unrelated platform redesign.

## LENS_EVIDENCE_TO_INSPECT

Locate the source policy or legal interpretation, system inventory, data flows, customer agreements, prior assessments, incident history, and owners accountable for interpretation. Distinguish verified obligations from assumptions and from desirable risk reduction. Record the scope of persons, geography, data, or workflow the obligation actually reaches.

## LENS_RECOMMENDATIONS_TO_MAKE

Recommend the observable product behavior, control objective, customer or operator communication, evidence needed to show compliance, and a constrained scope. Explain how the change protects affected parties while preserving the intended product outcome. Include a measured guardrail for accessibility, fairness, privacy, or service continuity when it is relevant.

## LENS_BLOCKING_PRODUCT_DECISIONS

Seek authority for interpretation disputes, risk acceptance, deadline tradeoffs, customer notification, data-use changes, or commitments that require legal, security, or accountable-owner approval. A packet must include Product0's recommendation, evidence or assumption, why the decision matters, and the minimum response needed to continue.

## LENS_IMPLEMENTATION_DETAILS_NOT_TO_ASK

Do not ask about encryption libraries, retention jobs, consent-widget code, logging formats, access-control middleware, scan tooling, or deployment configuration unless the mechanism changes the approved obligation. Technical brainstorming determines the implementation evidence and controls.
