# Product direction lenses

Use only the primary lens and any genuinely necessary secondary lens. These are prompts for professional judgment, not mandatory brief sections.

## Marketing or conversion surface

Resolve:

- target buyer and buying situation;
- page or surface job;
- desired perception after the first interaction;
- positioning and category language;
- value proposition and differentiation;
- message hierarchy;
- credible proof and trust signals;
- likely objections and how the surface answers them;
- product demonstration or visual storytelling;
- primary and secondary conversion;
- measurement and guardrails;
- claims that are confirmed, provisional, or unsafe.

Do not let form mechanics dominate this lens.

## Product workflow

Resolve:

- actor, trigger, goal, and end state;
- lifecycle and meaningful product-visible states;
- business rules and authority;
- consequential exceptions and recovery;
- permissions and visibility;
- operator/support implications;
- audit or evidence needs;
- scope boundaries.

Do not enumerate every routine validation state unless it changes product behavior.

## Platform capability

Resolve:

- consumer and job to be done;
- promised capability and value;
- configuration versus customization;
- ownership and control boundaries;
- guarantees and limits;
- extension and integration expectations;
- operational visibility;
- adoption and migration implications.

## Integration

Resolve:

- parties and their product responsibilities;
- user or operator outcome;
- data and control ownership;
- permissions and consent;
- failure expectations visible outside the implementation;
- reconciliation and operator visibility;
- commercial, privacy, and compliance constraints;
- boundaries that remain technical choices.

## Pricing or packaging

Resolve:

- buyer and value metric;
- billable unit;
- package boundaries and entitlements;
- transparency and comparability;
- incentives and likely objections;
- provisional versus approved commercial claims;
- exceptions, commitments, and guardrails.

Never convert “likely,” “expected,” or “target” pricing into a fixed public claim.

## Internal operation

Resolve:

- operator and decision or task;
- current friction and evidence;
- target operational outcome;
- handoffs and authority;
- exception handling;
- visibility, audit, and escalation;
- measurable operational improvement.

## Compliance-driven change

Resolve:

- source and status of the obligation;
- affected product behavior and actors;
- required evidence or controls;
- consent, disclosure, retention, and access expectations;
- exceptions and enforcement timing;
- which details require legal or compliance confirmation.

## Product quality or usability

Resolve:

- observed user pain and supporting evidence;
- desired experience;
- affected journey and frequency;
- severity and business impact;
- behavior that must change;
- guardrails and measures;
- whether the issue is product direction or implementation quality.
